What is the Midwest Cup?

The Midwest Cup is a golf tournament that brings the best players in the central US together to compete in an interstate team event. The mission of the Midwest Cup is to develop and foster goodwill between the participating states and raise money for juniors in golf. We are a registered 501 (c)(3) charitable organization. The tournament committee has earmarked "The Evans Scholarship Fund" and various other golf associations junior programs as recipients.

The tournament is rotated around the midwest among the participating states. Players are chosen from the state golf association points list, with captains from each team determining who to invite and how the players will be paired against one another. The goal of the event is to become the premier amateur event in the Midwest. In addition to the team event, there is a 54 hole individual competition with the winners getting exemptions into the North/South Amateurs and other regional tournaments.
